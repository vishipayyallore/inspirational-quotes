### 1. Quick Centering (1 minute)

- Take three deep breaths and read today’s intention aloud.
- Intention prompt: *“I learn English with clarity and confidence.”*

### 2. Vocabulary Boost (3 minutes)

- **Word:** Transcend
- **Meaning:** To go beyond the range or limits of something; to surpass.
- **Usage in context:** "To grow, you must transcend your current limitations and embrace the unknown."
- **Micro-practice (1 min):** Say "transcend" aloud three times. Sentence: "I want to transcend my fears."
- **Reflection (1 min):** Think of one limit you would like to transcend this year.

### 3. Core Grammar Drill (4 minutes)

- **Grammar concept:** Simple Past Tense
- **Essentials:**
  - Rule summary: Use the past form of the verb for actions completed in the past. Regular verbs add "-ed".
  - Key pattern / formula: Subject + Verb-ed (or irregular past form).

- **Mini-drills (4 x 45 sec):**
 1. Read the sample: "I transcended my expectations yesterday."
 2. Transform to negative: "I did not transcend my expectations yesterday."
 3. Transform to a question: "Did I transcend my expectations yesterday?"
 4. Create your own sentence: "Last year, she transcended the difficulties she faced."

### 4. Integration Wrap-Up (1 minute)

- Record one complete sentence that uses "transcend" and the simple past tense.
- Example: "He transcended his previous record during the competition."
